Few-shot Action Recognition

üìù Background:

In the rapidly evolving field of Artificial Intelligence, especially within the subset of computer vision, there's a noticeable shift from reliance on data-driven approaches towards more intelligent and autonomous learning mechanisms. Despite significant strides achieved by deep learning in action recognition, a major bottleneck remains: a heavy dependency on large-scale annotated datasets. This dependency not only incurs substantial manpower costs but also limits the applicability of these models in critical areas like defense, military, and medical diagnostics, where acquiring and annotating data is particularly challenging. This project explores few-shot learning as a solution, inspired by the human ability to recognize and classify new actions with minimal examples.

Multi-modal Self-supervised Contrastive Learning for Few-Shot Action Recognition:

This work introduces an innovative few-shot action recognition method based on multi-modal self-supervised contrastive learning. By leveraging a novel contrastive learning strategy that utilizes comparisons between different modalities, the model can learn deeper representational information. Furthermore, considering the importance of spatial features, a four-dimensional attention module was designed to enhance the model‚Äôs capability in processing spatial features by cross-enhancing features in different spatial dimensions. Experiments have demonstrated the effectiveness of this approach in few-shot action recognition tasks, with the contrastive learning module showing significant performance advantages under conditions of relatively abundant samples.

Hybrid Attention Prototypical Networks for Few-Shot Action Recognition (Under Submission):

Current models for few-shot action recognition tend to decouple the processing of temporal and spatial information from action videos, leading to insufficient integration of spatiotemporal features. Given the continuous sequence nature of videos and their inherently coupled spatiotemporal information, decoupled processing risks losing essential information and breaking the intrinsic connections of temporal and spatial dimensions. This work proposes a novel approach utilizing R(2+1)D networks to transform video sequences into joint spatiotemporal feature representations. A residual three-dimensional attention module collaboratively processes channel, spatial, and temporal dimensions. To mitigate overfitting issues common in few-shot scenarios, a prototypical attention-matching module is introduced to enhance the model‚Äôs generalization ability. Extensive experiments across classic few-shot action recognition datasets have showcased the superior performance and robustness of this method.
